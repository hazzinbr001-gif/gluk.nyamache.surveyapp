The App
Community Health Survey v2.0 is a free, offline-first Progressive Web App (PWA) built specifically for community health fieldwork at Great Lakes University of Kisumu, in collaboration with Nyamache Sub County Hospital, Kisii County, Kenya.

It was designed so that student health workers can collect data in the field — even with zero internet — and generate official Ministry of Health format reports automatically. No paper, no spreadsheets, no stress. 🎉
The Developer

This app was built with genuine care for public health in Kenya, powered by too much coffee ☕, a love for clean code, and the firm belief that community health workers deserve better tools than a clipboard and a prayer.

Absolutely yes. This app was built to be adaptable. It can be customised for any community health survey, any county, any university, or any public health programme in Kenya or beyond. Different survey sections, different questions, different report formats — all possible. Just reach out. The goal is better public health data for everyone. 🌍

🆘 Help & Contact
Quick Help
🔑 Login Issues
Use your admission number in the format given by your university (e.g. B11/GLUK/S53K/2022). Make sure you are connected to the internet the first time you register. If your access has been removed, contact your course coordinator.
📴 Working Offline
The app works fully offline. All your interviews are saved on your phone automatically. When you get back to an area with internet, tap Upload Data to sync your records to the server.
📑 Generating Reports
After finishing an interview, tap Finish and choose your report type. For all your reports together, tap My Reports on the home page. To print as PDF, open the report then use your browser's Share → Print → Save as PDF.
🔄 App Not Updating?
If the app looks different from what others have, close it completely and reopen. The app updates itself in the background when you have internet. If still stuck, go to your browser settings → Clear site data → reopen.
Contact — Course Coordinator
For academic issues, access problems, or survey-related questions, contact your course coordinator at Great Lakes University of Kisumu or report to the health management team at Nyamache Sub County Hospital.
